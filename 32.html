Fundamental Strategic Design Needed
What tools are available with DDD to help us avoid such pitfalls? You need at least two fundamental
strategic design tools. One is the
Bounded Context
and the other is the
Ubiquitous Language.
Employing a
Bounded Context
forces us to answer the question “What is core?” The
Bounded
Context
should hold closely all concepts that are core to the strategic initiative and push out all
others. The concepts that remain are part of the team’s
Ubiquitous Language.
You will see how
DDD works to avoid the design of monolithic applications.
Testing Benefits
Because
Bounded Contexts
are not monolithic, other benefits are experienced when they
are used. One such benefit is that tests will be focused on one model and thus be fewer in
number and will run more quickly. Although this isn’t the primary motivation to use
Bounded Contexts
, it sure pays off in other ways.
Literally, some concepts will be in context and be clearly included in the team’s language.
