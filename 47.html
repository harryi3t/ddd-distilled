Another comment on microservices is in order. Some consider a microservice to be much smaller
than a DDD
Bounded Context.
Using that definition, a microservice models only one concept and
manages one narrow type of data. An example of such a microservice is a
Product
and another is a
BacklogItem
. If this is the granularity that you consider a worthy microservice, understand that
both the
Product
microservice and the
BacklogItem
microservice will still be in the same
larger, logical
Bounded Context.
The two small microservice components have only different
deployment units, which may also have an impact on how they interact (see
Context Mapping
).
Linguistically they are still within the same Scrum-based contextual and semantic boundary.
