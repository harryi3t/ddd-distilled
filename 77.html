Rule 2: Design Small Aggregates
This rule highlights that the memory footprint and transactional scope of each
Aggregate
should be
relatively small. In the preceding diagram the
Aggregate
that is represented is not small. Here,
Product
literally contains a potentially very large collection of
BacklogItem
instances, a large
collection of
Release
instances, and a large collection of
Sprint
instances. Over time, these
collections could grow to be quite large, with thousands of
BacklogItem
instances and probably
hundreds of
Release
and
Sprint
instances. This design approach is generally a very poor choice.
